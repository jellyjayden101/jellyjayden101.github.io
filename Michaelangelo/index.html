Why is Michelangelo signifigant

Michelangelo was a sculptor, painter, and architect who is regarded as one of the greatest artists of all time and one of the most well-known Renaissance painters. His creations revealed a confluence of psychological knowledge, physical reality, and passion that had never before been witnessed.

Timeline Of Michelangelo

1485

Michelangelo Attended School

Michelangelo attended school. He was taught by a humanist, Francesco da Urbino till 1488. Michelangelo's father remarried a woman named Lucrezia Ubaldini.

1487

Michelangelo Became An Apprentise

He became an apprentice to the painter Domenico Ghirlandaio

1490 - 1492

Worked for the Medici

Michelangelo worked in the Medici family

1494 - 1495

He worked in Bologna

Michelangelo worked in Bologna

1495

St John the Baptist and the Sleeping Cupid

He returned to Florence where he carved St John the Baptist and the Sleeping Cupid

1496-1501

Bacchus and the Rome Pietà

He moved to Rome where he carved the Bacchus and the Rome Pietà

1501

Piccolomini altar and David

Returned to Florence. Contracted to carve figures for the Piccolomini altar and received the commission to carve the David

1504

David and Battle of Cascina

Completed the David. Received commission to paint the Battle of Cascina

1505

Summoned by the Pope

He's summoned to Rome by Pope Julius II. He is commissioned to create the tomb for the pope. Michelangelo spent 8 months in the quarries selecting a marble for the tomb.

1508-1512

Sistine Chapel

In Rome painting the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel (finished October 1512)
